Shift the emissions from an Observable forward in time by a particular amount. 

The delay operator modifies its source Observable by pausing for a particular increment of time (that you specify) before emitting each of the source Observable’s items. This has the effect of shifting the entire sequence of items emitted by the Observable forward in time by that specified increment.




\begin{DoxyParams}{Parameters}
{\em delay\+\_\+duration} & is the delay duration for emitting items. Delay duration should be able to cast to rpp\+::schedulers\+::duration. \\
\hline
{\em scheduler} & provides the threading model for delay. e.\+g. With a new thread scheduler, the observer sees the values in a new thread after a delay duration to the subscription. \\
\hline
\end{DoxyParams}

new \mbox{\hyperlink{classrpp_1_1specific__observable}{specific\+\_\+observable}} with the delay operator as most recent operator.
